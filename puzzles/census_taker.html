🎯 Census-Taker Problem

A classic logic puzzle: three children, a house number, and one extra hint about the oldest child.

Time
00:00

During a recent census, a man told the census taker that he had three children. When asked their ages, he replied, “The product of their ages is 72. The sum of their ages is the same as my house number.” The census taker ran to the door and looked at the house number.

“I still can’t tell,” she complained. “Oh, that’s right. I forgot to tell you that the oldest one likes apple pie.” The census taker promptly wrote down the ages of the three children.

How old are the three children?

Enter the three ages

Type the ages as whole numbers in years. Order does not matter.

Rules & Hints

  • There are exactly three children, and their ages are whole numbers in years.
  • The product of their ages is 72.
  • The sum of their ages matches the house number on the door.
  • Even after seeing the house number, the census taker still could not tell their ages — there must be more than one possible triple with that sum.
  • One extra clue: there is a single oldest child (who likes apple pie!).

Use the clues together to narrow down the possibilities, then enter the only triple that fits all of them.
